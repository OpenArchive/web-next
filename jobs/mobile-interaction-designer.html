Job Opening

Mobile Interaction Designer

About OpenArchive

OpenArchive is an experienced research and development nonprofit organization dedicated to the ethical collection and long-term preservation of mobile media. We work to promote freedom of expression and privacy on the internet by creating tools, guides, and workshops to protect human rights defenders and their media. Our work centers both large human rights focused NGOs as well as activist communities, researchers, designers, developers, academic institutions, and advocates to create and share best practices in order to advance secure, long-term mobile media preservation and internet freedom for marginalized and targeted communities.

About the Position

The mobile interaction designer will work closely with the Tech Lead and the Executive Director to guide the usability, design and feature improvements of our app Save. We expect the designer to have a robust toolkit and the flexibility to adapt to new technologies, in addition to having an evolved understanding of how people use their devices and how they use and want to use the Save app.

Usability is key when building secure tools as there is always a security-usability trade-off. This role will help us improve the usability and accessibility of our secure mobile archiving application Save so that it continues to be a leading human rights tool that prioritizes usability as much as privacy. Our larger goal is to show that secure tools can be so easy to use, they become the rule and not the exception. We strive to regularly incorporate new ux research findings into the app every few months, so this work will be ongoing and iterative.

The mobile interaction designer will work with the Executive, Tech, and QA teams to identify user flow / ux frictions in both Android and iOS versions of Save. They will then offer ux and design solutions, reporting back to our Tech Lead and the Executive Director. After critical user pathways are addressed, this role will then work with the Executive and Tech teams to create a spec / roadmap of improvements to implement as we further decentralize and add new features to the app.

Responsibilities

  • Create a highly usable and intuitive mobile interface that is brand-consistent across all mobile experiences;
  • Develop intuitive, usable, and engaging interactions and visual designs for Save;
  • Provide strategic design thinking and creative problem solving;
  • Collaborate with cross-functional teams throughout the design process;
  • Participate in the development process from definition, through design, build, test, release, and maintenance;
  • Break any design problem down into viable actionable chunks and solve them with clarity and precision;
  • Stay in the loop and on top of the latest UX trends, standards, changes, trends in the mobile design field and in parallel analogous worlds.

Required skills and experience

  • Strong graphic design skills, with a good understanding of typography, intuitive layouts and palate development;
  • A bachelor’s or higher degree in interaction design, human-computer interaction, cognitive science or related field. A master’s degree is preferred;
  • Strong ability to recognize and predict use-cases and user interaction, including happy path, edge and corner cases, and incorporate them into designs;
  • Excellent understanding of user-experience design for mobile and the web, technology trends, and demonstrable design skills;
  • A collaborative person who can easily adapt in a rapidly changing environment;
  • Expert level skills in relevant interaction design tools (e.g. Photoshop, InvisionApp, UXPin);
  • Deep understanding of Apple’s human interface design guidelines, Android design and other mobile platform designs, including Google’s material design.

Working with us

OpenArchive aims to empower people to have more agency over the long-term preservation, privacy, management, and access to their mobile media. We are working on challenging, but compelling problems at the crux of privacy, usability, and long-term preservation of mobile media. If you are passionate about helping people create secure, robust, personal archives to preserve mobile media, especially media made by historically marginalized communities, or are just interested in making secure archiving tools more usable, we’d love to hear from you.

Reporting

This position reports to the OpenArchive Tech Lead and Executive Director.

Location

This position is remote. OpenArchive operates as a distributed team, with core staff based in the U.S., and others based in Africa, Latin America, and Europe. Overlap with US time-zones is preferred.

Compensation

This is a contract position for 100-150 hours through December 31st, 2022 at a competitive rate depending on experience. This role will continue through 2024 on a flexible part-time hourly contract.
